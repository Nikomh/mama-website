## Tone of Voice

### Core Qualities
- **Warm** — like a reassuring conversation, not a brochure
- **Clear** — plain language; no clinical jargon
- **Grounded** — steady and calm, not salesy or urgent
- **Empathetic** — acknowledge the courage it takes to seek help

### Words We Use
present, safe, understood, real, gentle, together, at your pace, human

### Words We Avoid
diagnose, disorder, treatment, patient, case, intervention, optimized, results

---

## Writing Principles

1. **Lead with the client's experience, not the therapist's credentials.**
   - ✗ "With 15 years of experience, I offer evidence-based therapy..."
   - ✓ "If you've been carrying something heavy, you don't have to carry it alone."

2. **Short paragraphs.** 2-3 sentences max per block. White space = breathing room.

3. **Scannable structure.** Use subheadings, bullets, and short sections.

4. **CTAs feel like an invitation, not a push.**
   - ✗ "Book your session today"
   - ✓ "Let's have a conversation" / "Reach out — no commitment"

5. **Address fears directly.**
   Common barriers: "I don't know if I'm 'bad enough' to need therapy" / "What do I even say?" / "What if I cry?" / "Is this confidential?"

6. **SEO-aware but human first.** Include natural mentions of specialty + location. Never stuff keywords.

## Page-by-Page Outline

### Homepage
- **Hero:** 1 powerful line that speaks to the client's feeling + CTA
- **Intro:** 2-3 sentences about the practice — warmth, not bio
- **Services preview:** 3 cards max, each with an icon and 1-line description
- **About teaser:** 2 sentences + photo + link to About page
- **Testimonials / social proof:** optional, if client has them
- **CTA section:** low-friction invitation to reach out

### About
- Personal story (humanizing, not exhaustive)
- Approach + philosophy
- Credentials (brief, at the bottom)
- Photo

### Services
- Each service gets: name, who it's for, what to expect, CTA
- Keep it client-benefit focused, not technique-focused

### Contact
- Warm intro: "Taking this step is brave..."
- Form: name, email, brief message, preferred contact method
- What to expect after submitting (response time)
- Phone / address / map if in-person offered

### FAQ
- 6-10 questions max
- Written in the client's voice ("Do I have to talk about my childhood?")
- Answers are reassuring and direct

### Fees & Insurance _(if applicable)_
- Clear, transparent pricing
- Address insurance honestly
- Note sliding scale if offered
